How the System Works

The Insight Forge System

You generate intellectual property every single day through your practice, your client calls, your reflections, and your teaching. Most of it disappears. This system captures it, distills it, and turns it into content and offers.

The system has three layers: Capture (recording your insights), Distill (extracting the signal from the noise), and Create (turning distilled concepts into content, trainings, and offers).

Daily Practice
1

Do Your Practice

Tarot reading, breathwork, coaching session, technique practice, client call. Whatever your daily practice looks like, do it with awareness that insights will surface.

2

Record an Insight Loom

Spend 5 to 10 minutes talking through the insight that emerged. What was it? What triggered it? How have you seen this show up before, in your own journey or with students? What is the practical application? What framework does it connect to? Drop the Loom link into your NotebookLM notebook.

3

Log It Here

If the insight feels significant, add it as a Concept in the Concepts tab. Tag the source, the connected framework, and its current readiness level. You can always come back and develop it further.

Weekly Distillation
1

Load Your Sources

Consolidate your daily Looms into one weekly Loom. Pull transcripts from any coaching or implementation calls. Drop them into your NotebookLM notebook.

2

Run the Extraction Prompts

Go to the Prompts tab and use the NotebookLM extraction prompts. Run them in order. Review the output.

3

Process into Concepts and Ideas

Take the most important findings and add them as Concepts and Ideas here. A concept is a theme or principle. An idea is a specific piece of content or action you could take.

4

Create from Your Vault

Use the Claude prompts to turn developed concepts into blog posts, social content, emails, trainings, or challenge material. Your vault feeds your content pipeline.

NotebookLM Extraction Prompts

Run these in your NotebookLM notebook after loading weekly sources. Copy each prompt, paste it in, and review the output before moving on.

Questions Asked

Review all call transcripts from this week. List every specific question a member asked or problem they brought to the call. For each one, include: - Who asked it (if identifiable) - The core question in their words - The underlying need behind the question (what they were really asking) - Which framework or concept it connects to

IP Generated

What advice, frameworks, analogies, step-by-step processes, or solutions were taught or explained during these calls? Focus on anything that was spontaneous or said in the moment rather than pre-planned. Include: - The core idea or framework articulated - The context that prompted it - A brief direct quote if the exact wording was strong - Whether this is a refinement of an existing framework or something new Flag anything that could become a standalone training, blog post, or curriculum addition.

Member Wins

Identify any breakthroughs, progress reports, celebrations, or aha moments that members shared during these calls. Note what specifically shifted for them and what it tells us about where the community is in their journey right now.

Insight Loom Inventory

Review the insight Loom(s) from this week. For each distinct insight discussed, create an entry with: - The core insight in one sentence - What triggered it (which student, which situation, which call moment) - Any personal story connected to it - Which existing framework it relates to or extends - The practical application (how would this actually help a student)

Weekly Patterns

Looking across ALL sources this week (calls AND insight Looms), what patterns do you see? Specifically: - What topics or themes came up more than once? - What are members collectively struggling with right now? - Are there any disconnects between what is being taught and what members are actually asking about? - What is the strongest content opportunity from this week?

Offer Intelligence

Based on everything from this week, are there any signals about: - Support or resources members are asking for that do not currently exist? - Things members would likely pay for based on the urgency or frequency of their questions? - Topics that could become a mini course, workshop, or sprint offer? - Gaps in the current offerings that these conversations reveal?

Search a Topic

Which Loom or call discussed [TOPIC]? Give me the source name, summarize what was said, and include any direct quotes that capture the core idea.

Blog Post from Concept

I want to write a blog post based on this concept: Concept: [CONCEPT TITLE] Core insight: [WHAT THE CONCEPT IS ABOUT] Personal story: [YOUR EXPERIENCE WITH THIS] Student example: [HOW YOU HAVE SEEN THIS SHOW UP FOR STUDENTS] Framework connection: [WHICH OF YOUR FRAMEWORKS THIS RELATES TO] Audience: [WHO THIS IS FOR] Write this in my brand voice. The tone should feel like a conversation between equals, not a lecture from above. Use the personal story and student example to ground it. End with a practical takeaway they can apply today.

Content Batch from Concept

I want to create a full content batch from this concept: Concept: [CONCEPT TITLE] Core insight: [WHAT THE CONCEPT IS ABOUT] Personal story: [YOUR EXPERIENCE WITH THIS] Student example: [HOW YOU HAVE SEEN THIS SHOW UP] Framework connection: [WHICH FRAMEWORK THIS RELATES TO] Create: 1. A blog post (600-800 words) 2. An email blast version (shorter, more personal, includes a CTA) 3. Five social media captions for the concept (follow my brand caption SOP)

Training Outline from Concept

I want to turn this concept into a training for my community: Concept: [CONCEPT TITLE] Core insight: [WHAT THE CONCEPT IS ABOUT] Why it matters: [WHY THIS IS IMPORTANT FOR PRACTITIONERS] Framework connection: [WHICH FRAMEWORK THIS RELATES TO] Examples: [STUDENT EXAMPLES OR YOUR OWN EXPERIENCE] Create a training outline that includes: - A compelling title - The key teaching points (3-5) - An interactive or reflective exercise - A practical action step they can take immediately after

Offer Idea Development

I have been noticing a pattern in my community that suggests an offer opportunity: The pattern: [WHAT YOU HAVE BEEN SEEING] Questions being asked: [SPECIFIC QUESTIONS RELATED TO THIS] Current gap: [WHAT IS MISSING FROM YOUR CURRENT OFFERINGS] Framework connection: [WHICH FRAMEWORK THIS RELATES TO] Help me explore what this offer could look like: - What would the transformation be? - What format fits (course, sprint, workshop, template, toolkit)? - What price point makes sense based on the depth? - What is the most compelling way to name and position it?
