How the System Works

The Insight Forge System

You generate intellectual property every single day through your practice, your client calls, your reflections, and your teaching. Most of it disappears. This system captures it, distills it, and turns it into content and offers.

The system has three layers: Capture (recording your insights), Distill (extracting the signal from the noise), and Create (turning distilled concepts into content, trainings, and offers).

The Two-Notebook Workflow

Weekly Forge (Processing Notebook)

This is your working notebook in NotebookLM. Every week you load it with fresh transcripts, run the extraction prompts, and process the output. Think of it as your workbench: things come in, get distilled, and move out.

Weekly cycle: Load transcripts at the start of your distillation session. Run all extraction prompts. Route the output to the right places. Then remove the transcripts from this notebook so it stays clean for the next week. Only the current week's sources should live here at any given time.

Insights Vault (Accumulation Notebook)

This is your long-term knowledge base. The best IP, framework refinements, and ad intelligence findings from each weekly processing session get documented here as dated entries. Over time this notebook becomes a searchable archive of everything your practice has produced.

What lives here permanently: Dated framework refinement entries, ad intelligence analysis, high-value IP discoveries, pattern summaries, and anything you want to reference or build on later. This notebook grows. The Weekly Forge stays lean.
Routing Workflow

Where Everything Goes

After running extraction prompts, route each type of output to its designated home:

IP Generated ➡ Concepts tab in The Insight Forge
Content Seeds ➡ Ideas tab in The Insight Forge
Framework Refinements ➡ Refinement Logs on the framework (Setup tab) + dated entry in Insights Vault notebook
Ad Intelligence ➡ Separate dated section in the Insights Vault notebook
Offer Signals ➡ Concepts tab (tagged as Pattern source) or Ideas tab (tagged as Offer type)
Daily Practice
1

Do Your Practice

Tarot reading, breathwork, coaching session, technique practice, client call. Whatever your daily practice looks like, do it with awareness that insights will surface.

2

Record an Insight Loom

Spend 5 to 10 minutes talking through the insight that emerged. What was it? What triggered it? How have you seen this show up before, in your own journey or with students? What is the practical application? What framework does it connect to?

3

Paste the Transcript

Copy the Loom transcript text and paste it directly into your Weekly Forge notebook as a source. Do not paste the Loom video link. NotebookLM needs the raw transcript text to analyze, not a URL it cannot access.

4

Log It Here

If the insight feels significant, add it as a Concept in the Concepts tab. Tag the source, the connected framework, and its current readiness level. Flag it with the flame if it feels high-priority.

Weekly Distillation
1

Load Your Sources

Paste this week's Loom transcripts and any coaching or implementation call transcripts into your Weekly Forge notebook. Remember: paste the transcript text, not links to videos.

2

Run the Extraction Prompts

Go to the Prompts tab and use the NotebookLM extraction prompts. Run them in order. Review the output.

3

Route the Output

Follow the routing workflow above. IP goes to Concepts. Content seeds go to Ideas. Framework refinements go to the Setup tab refinement logs and to a dated entry in your Insights Vault. Ad intelligence goes to its own dated section in the Insights Vault.

4

Clean the Weekly Forge

After processing, remove this week's transcripts from your Weekly Forge notebook. It should be empty and ready for next week's sources. Your Insights Vault keeps the accumulated knowledge.

5

Create from Your Vault

Use the Claude creation prompts to turn developed concepts into blog posts, social content, emails, trainings, or challenge material. Your vault feeds your content pipeline.

NotebookLM Extraction Prompts
How to use: Run these in your Weekly Forge notebook after loading this week's transcripts. Copy each prompt, paste it in, and review the output before moving on. Run them in order: each prompt is written to surface only new information not already covered by the previous prompts.

1. Questions Asked

Review all call transcripts from this week. List every specific question a member asked or problem they brought to the call. For each one, include: - Who asked it (if identifiable) - The core question in their words - The underlying need behind the question (what they were really asking) - Which framework or concept it connects to

2. IP Generated

What advice, frameworks, analogies, step-by-step processes, or solutions were taught or explained during these calls? Focus on anything that was spontaneous or said in the moment rather than pre-planned. Include: - The core idea or framework articulated - The context that prompted it - A brief direct quote if the exact wording was strong - Whether this is a refinement of an existing framework or something new Flag anything that could become a standalone training, blog post, or curriculum addition.

3. Member Wins

Identify any breakthroughs, progress reports, celebrations, or aha moments that members shared during these calls. Note what specifically shifted for them and what it tells us about where the community is in their journey right now.

4. Insight Loom Inventory

Review the insight Loom transcript(s) from this week. For each distinct insight discussed, create an entry with: - The core insight in one sentence - What triggered it (which student, which situation, which call moment) - Any personal story connected to it - Which existing framework it relates to or extends - The practical application (how would this actually help a student)

5. Gap Analysis and Offer Signals

Set aside everything already surfaced in the previous prompts (questions, IP, wins, Loom insights). Looking only at what has NOT been covered yet, identify: - Unspoken needs: things members are struggling with that they did not directly ask about but that showed up in how they described their situation - Gaps in current offerings: support, tools, or resources that do not currently exist but would clearly serve what this community needs right now - Offer signals: topics with enough urgency or frequency to justify a paid sprint, mini course, workshop, or new template - Pricing context: based on the depth of need and transformation implied, what format and price point would fit Do not repeat anything from prompts 1 through 4. This prompt is exclusively for net-new gaps and signals.

6. Ad Intelligence and Sales Page Comparison

Analyze any ad-related discussions, competitor mentions, or market observations from this week's sources. Extract: - Language patterns: specific words, phrases, or framings members used to describe their struggles or desires that would work well in ad copy or sales page headlines - Objection signals: hesitations, doubts, or fears members voiced that need to be addressed in sales copy - Competitor awareness: any mention of other programs, courses, or practitioners, and what members said they liked or did not like about them - Positioning gaps: areas where your approach clearly differs from what members have experienced elsewhere - Sales page comparison: based on the language members actually use versus the language on your current sales pages, where is there a mismatch between how you describe your offers and how your people describe what they need? Format this as a dated intelligence brief that can be saved to the Insights Vault notebook.

Blog Post from Concept

I want to write a blog post based on this concept: Concept: [CONCEPT TITLE] Core insight: [WHAT THE CONCEPT IS ABOUT] Personal story: [YOUR EXPERIENCE WITH THIS] Student example: [HOW YOU HAVE SEEN THIS SHOW UP FOR STUDENTS] Framework connection: [WHICH OF YOUR FRAMEWORKS THIS RELATES TO] Audience: [WHO THIS IS FOR] Write this in my brand voice. The tone should feel like a conversation between equals, not a lecture from above. Use the personal story and student example to ground it. End with a practical takeaway they can apply today.

A blog post (600-800 words) 2. An email blast version (shorter, more personal, includes a CTA) 3. Five social media captions for the concept (follow my brand caption SOP)

Training Outline from Concept

I want to turn this concept into a training for my community: Concept: [CONCEPT TITLE] Core insight: [WHAT THE CONCEPT IS ABOUT] Why it matters: [WHY THIS IS IMPORTANT FOR PRACTITIONERS] Framework connection: [WHICH FRAMEWORK THIS RELATES TO] Examples: [STUDENT EXAMPLES OR YOUR OWN EXPERIENCE] Create a training outline that includes: - A compelling title - The key teaching points (3-5) - An interactive or reflective exercise - A practical action step they can take immediately after

Offer Idea Development

I have been noticing a pattern in my community that suggests an offer opportunity: The pattern: [WHAT YOU HAVE BEEN SEEING] Questions being asked: [SPECIFIC QUESTIONS RELATED TO THIS] Current gap: [WHAT IS MISSING FROM YOUR CURRENT OFFERINGS] Framework connection: [WHICH FRAMEWORK THIS RELATES TO] Help me explore what this offer could look like: - What would the transformation be? - What format fits (course, sprint, workshop, template, toolkit)? - What price point makes sense based on the depth? - What is the most compelling way to name and position it?
